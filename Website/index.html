📜 GALAGA HISTORY

From arcade cabinet to modern web, follow the legendary journey of Galaga:

1981
Galaga Released
Namco releases Galaga in Japanese arcades. With simple gameplay and addictive mechanics, it becomes an instant classic. The game introduces the revolutionary concept of enemies with personality and formation diving attacks.
1981-1990
Golden Age of Arcades
Galaga becomes one of the most iconic arcade games ever. Millions of quarters spent in arcades worldwide. The game influences an entire generation of game designers and creates the template for fixed-shooter games.
1990-2020
Legacy & Ports
Galaga appears on every gaming platform: home consoles, mobile devices, emulators. The core gameplay proves timeless. Game designers still study its balance and pacing.
2024
Galaga Wars - Modern Remake
A new generation discovers Galaga through a Star Wars skin. Built with professional game development patterns, demonstrating how arcade classics can be modernized while maintaining pure gameplay mechanics. State machines, inheritance, data-driven design, and comprehensive error handling.

⚙️ GAME MECHANICS

Enemy Types

TIE Fighter: Standard enemy that appears in formations. Quick dives and moderate attack patterns. The backbone of Imperial forces.

TIE Interceptor: Faster variant with tighter formation control. Deadly in coordinated attacks.

Imperial Shuttle: Boss-type enemy that dives less frequently but can activate tractor beams to capture your ship. Highest point value.

Game States

The game flows through carefully orchestrated states: Attract Mode (title screen), Instructions, Player Message, Stage Message, Enemy Spawning, Active Gameplay, Results, and High Score Entry. Challenge Stages appear every 4 waves for bonus points.

Scoring System

Earn points for destroying enemies. Different point values based on enemy type and whether they're diving. Extra lives awarded at 20,000 and every 70,000 points. Challenge stages reward perfect bonus for 100% accuracy.
